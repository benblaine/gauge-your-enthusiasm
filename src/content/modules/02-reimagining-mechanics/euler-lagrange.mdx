# The Euler-Lagrange Equation

<WhimsyBreak character="fox" mood="contemplative">
"What if," the fox murmured to his pet fern, adjusting his velvet smoking
jacket, "instead of tracking every force on every particle... we just asked
nature what path it *prefers*?"
</WhimsyBreak>

The principle of least action says nature minimises the **action**

$$
S = \int_{t_1}^{t_2} L(q, \dot q, t)\, dt
$$

Setting the variation $\delta S = 0$ for arbitrary $\delta q$ that vanishes
at the endpoints gives the **Euler-Lagrange equation**:

$$
\frac{d}{dt}\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot q}\right) - \frac{\partial L}{\partial q} = 0
$$

For the simple pendulum, $L = \tfrac{1}{2} m L^2 \dot\theta^2 - m g L (1 - \cos\theta)$,
and Euler-Lagrange spits out $\ddot\theta + (g/L)\sin\theta = 0$. No free body
diagrams, no decomposed tensions, no choice of axes. The Lagrangian doesn't
care how you label the world.

Watch the kinetic, potential, and total energy at the bottom-right. Energy
$E = T + V$ is conserved when there's no damping — a direct consequence of
the time-independence of $L$ (Noether's theorem).

## From Lagrange to Hamilton

Define the conjugate momentum $p = \partial L / \partial \dot q$ and Legendre
transform:

$$
H(q, p) = p\dot q - L
$$

Hamilton's equations $\dot q = \partial H / \partial p$ and
$\dot p = -\partial H / \partial q$ are the same dynamics, now living on
**phase space** instead of configuration space. The geometric structure that
makes phase space *phase space* is the symplectic 2-form $\omega = dp \wedge dq$
— and that structure is what gets quantised when we replace
$\{q, p\}_{\rm PB} = 1$ with $[\hat q, \hat p] = i\hbar$.
