# A Tour of the Standard Model

<WhimsyBreak character="raccoon" mood="delighted">
"It's all just," the raccoon waved his glass at the city, "*one Lagrangian*.
Slightly long, but. One."
</WhimsyBreak>

The Standard Model is a gauge theory with gauge group

$$
G = SU(3)_C \times SU(2)_L \times U(1)_Y
$$

After electroweak symmetry breaking by the Higgs vacuum, this collapses to

$$
SU(3)_C \times U(1)_{\rm em}
$$

Each factor is a force:

| Factor | Force | Carriers |
| --- | --- | --- |
| $SU(3)_C$ | Strong (QCD) | 8 gluons |
| $SU(2)_L \times U(1)_Y$ | Electroweak | $W^\pm, Z^0, \gamma$ |
| $U(1)_{\rm em}$ (after EWSB) | Electromagnetism | photon |

The matter content is three generations of left-handed weak doublets and
right-handed singlets of quarks and leptons:

$$
\begin{pmatrix} u \\ d \end{pmatrix}_L, \;
\begin{pmatrix} \nu_e \\ e \end{pmatrix}_L, \;
u_R, \; d_R, \; e_R, \;\ldots
$$

## The Higgs trick

The Higgs scalar $\phi$ has potential

$$
V(\phi) = \mu^2 |\phi|^2 + \lambda |\phi|^4
$$

with $\mu^2 < 0$. The vacuum sits not at $\phi = 0$ but on the brim of the
Mexican hat. Three of the four broken generators give mass to $W^\pm, Z^0$
via the Higgs mechanism; one combination (the photon) corresponds to the
unbroken $U(1)_{\rm em}$ and stays massless.

## The Lagrangian, schematically

$$
\mathcal L_{\rm SM} = -\tfrac{1}{4} F^a_{\mu\nu} F^{a\,\mu\nu}
+ \bar\psi i \gamma^\mu D_\mu \psi
+ |D_\mu \phi|^2 - V(\phi) - (Y_{ij} \bar\psi_i \phi \psi_j + \text{h.c.})
$$

Four pieces: gauge kinetic, fermion kinetic with covariant derivatives,
Higgs kinetic + potential, and Yukawa terms that give fermions mass after
EWSB.

This Lagrangian fits on a coffee mug, and it accounts for everything in the
universe except gravity, dark matter, dark energy, the matter–antimatter
asymmetry, neutrino masses (mostly), the strong CP problem, and the
hierarchy problem. Otherwise: bulletproof.
