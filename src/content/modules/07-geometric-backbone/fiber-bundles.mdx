# Fiber Bundles & Connections

<WhimsyBreak character="fern" mood="delighted">
The fern, against all expectation, was beginning to understand. It was a
*plant*. It had no business understanding bundles. And yet.
</WhimsyBreak>

A **fiber bundle** is a space that locally looks like the product of two
spaces — a base $M$ and a fiber $F$ — but globally may twist. Formally:

- Total space $E$
- Base manifold $M$
- Projection $\pi : E \to M$
- Each $\pi^{-1}(x) \cong F$
- Local trivialisations $\phi_U : \pi^{-1}(U) \to U \times F$

A **principal $G$-bundle** $P \to M$ has fiber equal to a Lie group $G$,
acting on itself by right multiplication. The structure group encodes "how
much the fibers twist as you walk around in $M$."

## A connection is a way to differentiate

Given $P \to M$ and a representation of $G$, we get an **associated vector
bundle** $E = P \times_G V$. A **connection** on $P$ — equivalently, a
covariant derivative on $E$ — tells you how to compare values of a section
at infinitesimally separated points.

In a local trivialisation, the connection is represented by a Lie-algebra
valued 1-form $A = A^a_\mu T_a \, dx^\mu$, the **gauge potential**.

Its curvature is the Lie-algebra valued 2-form

$$
F = dA + A \wedge A
$$

In components: $F^a_{\mu\nu} = \partial_\mu A^a_\nu - \partial_\nu A^a_\mu + f^a{}_{bc} A^b_\mu A^c_\nu$.

## The Wu-Yang dictionary

This is the bilingual phrasebook between geometry and physics:

| Geometry | Physics |
| --- | --- |
| Principal bundle $P \to M$ | Gauge theory on spacetime $M$ |
| Connection 1-form $A$ | Gauge potential |
| Curvature $F = dA + A \wedge A$ | Field strength |
| Gauge transformation (change of section) | Gauge transformation $A \to g^{-1}Ag + g^{-1}dg$ |
| Section of associated bundle | Matter field |
| Covariant derivative $D = d + A$ | Minimal coupling |
| Holonomy $\oint A$ | Wilson loop / Aharonov-Bohm phase |

When Weinstein says "the Standard Model *is* geometry," this is what he
means: the entire formal apparatus of gauge theory is identical to the
formal apparatus of bundle geometry. Same equations, two languages.

GR uses *Riemannian* geometry on the tangent bundle of spacetime; gauge
theory uses *Ehresmannian* geometry on a principal bundle over spacetime.
Geometric Unity (Module 9) is one attempt to put both on the same footing.
