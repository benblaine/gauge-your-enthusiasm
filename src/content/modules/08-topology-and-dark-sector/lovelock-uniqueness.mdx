# Lovelock's Uniqueness Theorem

<WhimsyBreak character="fox" mood="smug">

**Lovelock (1971):** In four spacetime dimensions, the only divergence-free,
symmetric, rank-(0,2) tensor built from the metric and its first two
derivatives, linear in the second derivatives, is

$$
A_{\mu\nu} = a \, G_{\mu\nu} + b \, g_{\mu\nu}
$$

where $G_{\mu\nu}$ is the Einstein tensor and $a, b$ are constants.

Translation: in 4D, the Einstein field equations
$G_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda g_{\mu\nu} = (8\pi G/c^4) T_{\mu\nu}$ are essentially
the *only* possibility. You cannot improve them while staying within
"theories of $g$ alone."

## Why DESI matters

In March 2025, DESI's DR2 results showed dark energy may not be a strict
cosmological constant — the equation-of-state parameter $w(z)$ appears to
deviate from $-1$, with a $4.2\sigma$ preference for dynamical dark energy
parameterised as

$$
w(a) = w_0 + w_a (1 - a)
$$

If this holds, Lovelock's theorem is screaming at us: **you can no longer
fit dark energy into a theory of the metric alone.** A varying $\Lambda$
demands new field content — a scalar field (quintessence), modified gravity,
extra dimensions, *something* beyond $g_{\mu\nu}$.

This is the kind of moment Weinstein loves: the math has been telling us
this for fifty years. Now the data has caught up.
