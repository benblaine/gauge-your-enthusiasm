# The Aharonov-Bohm Effect

<WhimsyBreak character="robot" mood="confused">
"It's not the field," beeped the robot. "*It's not the field.* It's the
*potential.* I have been wrong about *everything.*"

The Aharonov-Bohm effect (1959) is the cleanest demonstration that the
gauge potential $A_\mu$ — supposedly "unphysical" because of gauge invariance
— carries observable physics that the field $F_{\mu\nu}$ does not.

## The setup

Send electrons through a double-slit. Behind the slits, place a long
solenoid carrying magnetic flux $\Phi_B$, oriented so that the magnetic field
$\vec B$ is *entirely confined inside the solenoid*. The electrons travel
through a region where $\vec B = 0$ and $\vec E = 0$.

Classically: nothing happens. There's no force. There's no field.

Quantum mechanically: the interference pattern shifts by

$$
\Delta \varphi = \frac{e}{\hbar} \oint_C \vec A \cdot d\vec\ell = \frac{e}{\hbar} \Phi_B
$$

where the loop $C$ encircles the solenoid. **The electrons "feel" the
solenoid even though they never enter the field region.**

## Geometry's revenge

Locally, $\vec A$ is a gauge object — you can shift it by a gradient and
nothing observable changes. *Globally*, the Wilson loop $\oint A$ around a
non-contractible loop is a **holonomy**, and holonomy is gauge-invariant.

Translated through the Wu-Yang dictionary: the electromagnetic field is the
curvature of a $U(1)$ connection over spacetime. Curvature is local. But
*topology* — which loops are contractible — is global, and connections
remember the topology of the space they live on.

## Weinstein's meta-point

Classical electromagnetism was considered "fully explored" for almost a
century. Maxwell wrote it down in 1865. The Aharonov-Bohm paper was 1959 —
and revealed that one of the most important features of the theory had been
sitting in plain sight, missed because it requires multiply-connected space
and quantum mechanics to even formulate.

If a theory as scrutinised as classical EM had a hidden gauge structure for
nearly a century, what hidden structure does GR have? What does the Standard
Model? This is the engine of Weinstein's habitual rhetorical move: *no
theory is ever fully explored*.
