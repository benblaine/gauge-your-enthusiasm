# ER = EPR

<WhimsyBreak character="fox" mood="contemplative">

In 1935 Einstein published two famous papers:

- **ER**: Einstein-Rosen bridges — non-traversable wormholes connecting
  distant regions of spacetime through the geometry of an eternal black
  hole.
- **EPR**: Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen — the "spooky" correlations of entangled
  particles.

In 2013 Maldacena and Susskind proposed the **ER = EPR conjecture**: every
maximally entangled pair of quantum systems is dual to a non-traversable
wormhole connecting them. Entanglement *is* a wormhole, in the geometry-as-
quantum-information sense.

## Ryu-Takayanagi: entanglement entropy as area

The first quantitative crack: the **Ryu-Takayanagi formula** (2006) for
holographic entanglement entropy:

$$
S(A) = \frac{\text{Area}(\gamma_A)}{4 G_N}
$$

where $\gamma_A$ is the minimal-area extremal surface in the bulk anchored
to the boundary region $A$. The von Neumann entropy of a boundary region
equals the area of a bulk surface — exactly the form of the Bekenstein-Hawking
entropy of a black hole, applied to *any* boundary region.

If RT-type relations hold beyond holography, **spacetime connectivity is
literally a re-description of quantum entanglement**. ER = EPR formalises
the conjecture that gravity might not be fundamental: it might emerge from
the entanglement structure of an underlying QFT.

## Tensor networks

MERA, PEPS, and other tensor network states give *concrete models* in which
spatial geometry emerges from the pattern of entanglement among local
degrees of freedom. The discrete tensor network approximates a hyperbolic
slice of AdS — the geometry the entanglement encodes.

## What it would mean

If spacetime is emergent, then "warp drives," "exotic matter," and the rest
of the speculative menu of Module 9 are not problems of *mechanics* — they
are problems of *quantum information*. The answer to "how do you bend
spacetime" might be "you re-engineer the entanglement pattern of the
relevant degrees of freedom." That's a different field of engineering than
the one we currently know how to do.

It also reframes the closing question of the podcast: if you have craft
that defy the laws of physics, do you call a physicist, or a *quantum
information theorist*?
